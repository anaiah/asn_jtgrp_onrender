I. SERVICES

The CONTRACTOR shall provide coordination and delivery services as Coordinator/ Person In Charge (PIC) for the transport of parcels and packages to be delivered to customers.

II. COMMISSION FEE

For payment of the services rendered, the CONTRACTOR hereby proposes a monthly commission fee arrangement, which proposal is hereby accepted by the COMPANY.

With respect to the monthly commission rate, CONTRACTOR shall charge the COMPANY a rate of ________________, subject to 2% withholding tax.

The CONTRACTOR agrees that the COMPANY, upon notice to CONTRACTOR, can modify or change the rate of the commission fee unilaterally, upon proper notice to CONTRACTOR, from time to time in accordance with the circumstances then prevailing at the time of the review. It is understood that COMPANY is working for a third-party client and thus any adjustments on the rates done by the latter will have to be correspondingly implemented by the Parties.

The CONTRACTOR shall endeavour to act efficiently and effectively as Coordinator/ Person In Charge (PIC) in the coordination of riders, drivers, and other independent contractors and the delivery of the parcels and packages to the customers within reasonable time in accordance with standard industry practice, otherwise, this may affect, after review, the amount of commission fee that the CONTRACTOR may receive from the COMPANY.

The CONTRACTOR is free to offer his or her services to other individuals or companies and does not necessarily render exclusive service to the COMPANY.

III. CONTRACTOR OBLIGATIONS AND RESPONSIBILITIES

The CONTRACTOR shall:

1. Use reasonable care and skill as Coordinator/ Person In Charge (PIC) of riders, drivers, and other independent contractors in connection with the coordination and delivery services to make sure that the parcels and packages are successfully and completely delivered to each customer within the reasonable time standard in the industry.

2. Provide the tools, materials, and equipment as Coordinator/ Person In Charge (PIC) in carrying out the coordination and delivery services.

3. Ensure the proper coordination/management of riders, drivers, and other independent contractors and the accomplishment and completion of the subject delivery service

4. Ensure to perform the following:

(a) Sources and recruits individuals who are interested to become riders, drivers, and other independent contractors engaged in the delivery of parcels of Client Stick to a schedule and be time-bound on the job;

(b) Conducts orientation riders, drivers, and other independent contractors for the structure of the company;

(c) Submits report of actual deployment and newly hired;

(d) Provides assistance for newly deployed riders;

(e) Cascades instructions/ information given by Asian Now’s Head of Operations to riders, drivers, sorters;

(f) Guides the riders, drivers, and other independent contractors in the performance of their contracts with Asia-Now;

(g) Monitors attendance and day-today activities of riders, drivers, and other independent contractors, including loading and dispatching of parcels, delivery of parcels to Client’s customers, remittance of cash, and return of undelivered parcels to Client Hub;

(h) Consolidates concerns, issues and complaints received from client, riders, drivers, sorters and other independent contractors and coordinates with different departments of Asia Now; and Client -Hub Lead, Team Lead, Coordinator and Admins;

(i) Assists and coordinates required help relating to accident and life insurance

(j) Tracks riders with unremitted cash collections and assists on filing of case and report;

(k) Makes recommendation to Asia-Now in disciplinary cases;

(l) Provides assistance to client’s needs


IV. COMPANY OBLIGATIONS AND RESPONSIBILITIES

The COMPANY shall:

1. Provide CONTRACTOR schedules for the delivery service to customers.

2. Provide necessary documents to CONTRACTOR in connection with the delivery service.

3. Provide Service Fee charged by CONTRACTOR as specified in Section II.

The parties acknowledge that in connection with CONTRACTOR’S services, the COMPANY may disclose to CONTRACTOR confidential and proprietary information and trade secrets of the COMPANY, and that CONTRACTOR may also create such information within the scope and in the course of performing the services. CONTRACTOR hereby agrees that during the term of this agreement, CONTRACTOR shall not publicly divulge, disseminate, publish or otherwise disclose any Company Confidential Information without the Company’s prior written consent.

VI. DATA PRIVACY

The COMPANY collects personal information of customers necessary to perform its functions. The COMPANY and the CONTRACTOR agrees in committing to protect the privacy of customer’s information. We are required by law to protect personal information and comply with the Data Privacy Act (DPA) and other relevant legislation relating to confidentiality and privacy.

VII. NO EMPLOYER-EMPLOYEE RELATIONSHIP

CONTRACTOR acknowledges and agrees that there is no employer-employee relationship between him/her and the COMPANY. CONTRACTOR is hereby considered as an Independent Contractor. As such, standard statutory requirements prescribed for full-time employment are not applicable to this engagement. However, COMPANY reserves the exclusive discretion to grant the CONTRACTOR certain incentives. Should COMPANY opt to grant the CONTRACTOR incentives or any other remuneration, in whatever form, in addition to the CONTRACTOR’s service fee, such grant is considered an act of goodwill on the part of COMPANY and shall NOT in any way: (i) affect or elevate the status beyond the nature and status of CONTRACTOR granted by this Agreement, (ii) automatically vest as a continuous obligation on the part of COMPANY thereby COMPANY can cease the grant of any extra remuneration/benefit/privilege/incentives at any time without prior notice; and (iii) be deemed as a waiver by COMPANY of any right afforded to it relating to the consultancy status granted by this Agreement.

VIII. FORCE MAJEURE

Neither party shall be liable to the other for failure to perform due to causes beyond its reasonable control including, but not limited to, acts of God, acts of civil authorities, acts of military authorities, riots, embargoes, acts of nature and natural disasters, and other acts which may be due to unforeseen events or though foreseen could not be reasonably avoided.

IX. SEVERABILITY

The invalidity of any portion of this Agreement will not and shall not be deemed to affect the validity of any other provision. In the event that any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid, the COMPANY and the CONTRACTOR agree that the remaining provisions shall be deemed to be in full force and effect as if they had been executed subsequent to the expungement of the invalid provision.

X. TERM

Engagement Status. The duration of this Independent Service Agreement shall be co-terminus with the Service Agreement of the Employer with the Principal unless terminated for any cause as may be provided by law, rules and regulations of the proper authorities. The contractor shell be entitled to the prevailing commission rate at the time of the execution of this agreement unless otherwise provided modified by a subsequent law, rules and r egulations or agreed upon by parties in writing
This Agreement may be terminated at any time by the COMPANY, with or without cause, while CONTRACTOR may terminate the same upon thirty (30) days prior written notice to the COMPANY.

XI. ENTIRETY OF AGREEMENT

This Agreement represents the entire agreement between the COMPANY and the CONTRACTOR and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, agreements, either oral or written.

XII. AMENDMENTS AND MODIFICATIONS

This Agreement may be amended only by a written instrument signed and agreed upon by both parties.

XIII. COUNTERPARTS

This Agreement may be executed in two or more counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original, but all of which together shall constitute one and the same instrument.

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed according to the laws of the Republic of the Philippines. Should there be any dispute or action that arise from this agreement, it shall be understood that the venue for the said action or dispute if before the regular courts of Quezon City.
